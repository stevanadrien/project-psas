Saturn

Saturn, the sixth planet from the Sun, stands out as a breathtaking jewel in our cosmic neighborhood. Recognized for its stunning system of rings, Saturn is a gas giant, primarily composed of hydrogen and helium. Its sheer beauty and unique features have captivated astronomers and space enthusiasts alike. The most distinctive feature of Saturn is its magnificent ring system, a dazzling display of icy particles and rock fragments orbiting the planet. The rings are divided into several main groups, each named with letters of the alphabet in order of their discovery. These rings, composed mostly of water ice, reflect sunlight, creating a mesmerizing spectacle visible even from telescopes on Earth.

ATMOSPHERE

75% hydrogen and 25% helium with traces of other substances like methane and water ice.

CLIMATE

The temperature on the planet can range from -178 degrees Celsius at the upper cloud tops to over 11,000 degrees Celsius at the core.

PHYSICAL

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second-largest planet in our solar system.Saturn is not the only planet to have rings, but none are as spectacular or as complex as Saturn's.
