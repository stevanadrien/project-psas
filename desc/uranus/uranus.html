Uranus

Composed of hydrogen, helium, and methane, Uranus has a bluish hue due to the presence of methane in its atmosphere. The planet is encircled by a system of faint rings and rotates on its side, presenting an unusual orientation. Despite its distance, Uranus has been visited by spacecraft, providing valuable insights into its composition and atmosphere. The study of Uranus contributes to our understanding of planetary formation and the dynamics of distant worlds.
